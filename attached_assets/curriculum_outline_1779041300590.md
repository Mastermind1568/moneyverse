# Moneyverse Bitcoin Curriculum: Beginner to Intermediate

## Phase 1: The Foundation (Beginner)
- **What is Money?**: History of money, from barter to gold to fiat.
- **The Fiat Problem**: Inflation, central banking, and the "melting" of savings.
- **Bitcoin Basics**: What is Bitcoin? Who is Satoshi? The 21 million cap.
- **How it Works**: Blocks, miners, and the blockchain (non-technical).

## Phase 2: The Protocol (Beginner-Intermediate)
- **Scarcity & The Halving**: Understanding the 4-year cycle and supply shocks.
- **Wallets & Security**: Hot vs. Cold storage. "Not your keys, not your coins."
- **Buying Your First Satoshis**: Choosing an exchange, KYC, and making a purchase.
- **Self-Custody Execution**: Setting up a hardware wallet and securing seed phrases.

## Phase 3: The Strategy (Intermediate)
- **DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)**: Building a position without timing the market.
- **Cycle Theory**: Accumulation, Bull, and Bear phases.
- **Exit Architecture**: Setting price targets and managing emotions.
- **Advanced Security**: Multisig, inheritance planning, and physical security.

## Phase 4: The Global Context (Intermediate)
- **Bitcoin as a Macro Asset**: Correlation with M2, gold, and stocks.
- **Monetary Sovereignty**: Bitcoin in the developing world (Africa, Latin America).
- **The Future of the Standard**: Hyperbitcoinization and the circular economy.
